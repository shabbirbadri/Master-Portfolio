Chapter One

Who I Am

Not easily defined by a single profession, field, or title — and that's the point.

I am not easily defined by a single profession, academic field, or title. While formally pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Biotechnology, my intellectual interests extend across medicine, neuroscience, engineering, artificial intelligence, public health, education, history, literature, and leadership.

What distinguishes me isn't expertise confined to one domain — it's an unusual tendency to find connections between fields that are traditionally treated as separate. Biology informs engineering. History informs leadership. Literature informs communication. Technology informs education. Service informs innovation.

"How do complex systems work, and how can they be improved?"

That question sits underneath almost everything I do — in a lab, in a classroom, on a Scout campground, in an editor's chair, or behind a camera. People call me THK, The Human Kaleidoscope, for the way distinct interests refract into one focused direction rather than competing with each other.
