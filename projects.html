Chapter Four

Projects & Innovation

Three projects, three domains — and one long-term thesis underneath all of them.

Flagship Project · 01

CSF Pulmo-Somatic Oscillator

An interdisciplinary therapeutic concept exploring mechano-therapeutic approaches to neurodegenerative disorders — combining biotechnology, human physiology, medical engineering, neurological science, and rehabilitation design into a single device concept.

More than a device concept, this is the earliest real proof-of-concept for a much longer goal: founding biotechnology hubs in Pakistan. It's the first attempt at translating scientific understanding — physiology, engineering, and neuroscience together — into something with practical therapeutic value, rather than keeping the disciplines in separate boxes.

Functional Foods · 02

SynBioNutra — Peanut Butter Initiative

A food-science and product-development project built in collaboration with industry partners, exploring functional foods and nutritional enhancement from formulation through to process optimization.

Working directly with industrialists gave this project a commercial edge alongside the science — testing not just whether a formulation works, but whether it scales.

Documentary & Research · 03

Evolution of Pakistan Studies

A historical research and documentary project examining how Pakistan Studies has developed as an academic discipline — research, interviews, and film in one production.

Covers historical research, curriculum analysis, interview design, data collection, documentary scripting, video production and editing, and public presentation of the finished piece — the humanities counterpart to the lab-based projects.

Alongside these three, ongoing scientific research activity includes literature review and manuscript work, bioinformatics analysis (BLAST searches, primer design, sequence interpretation), scientific poster preparation, and conference participation.
