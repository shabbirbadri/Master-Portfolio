Chapter Seven

Philosophy

Six ideas that sit underneath the work, in my own words.

On Knowledge

Knowledge is not a collection of facts. Knowledge is the ability to understand relationships.

On Education

Education should not merely prepare people for examinations. Education should prepare people for reality.

On Leadership

Leadership is service. A leader is not the person with the most authority — a leader is the person most willing to accept responsibility.

On Innovation

The most powerful ideas often appear when two unrelated fields collide. The future belongs to those willing to connect disciplines.

On Failure

Failure is information. Every unsuccessful attempt reveals something that was previously unknown.

On Curiosity

Every discovery begins with a question. Every invention begins with dissatisfaction. Every breakthrough begins with wondering: what if?
